Research Report

Background Research

The purpose of this research is to streamline emergency ambulance services, focusing on professional medical staff to identify their needs and wants. Research was divided into three core groups as a team; regular users, caretakers and professional medical staff as by identifying common issues within groups was the most effective method in identifying a solution. This is especially important in ambulance services that rely on not only the medical professionals but also the users for a smooth operation.

To gain context on the chosen field, brief research on existing autonomous technology for medicine was conducted with majority of technology being drone delivery systems. Results highlighted the fact that medical emergencies were time critical, thus all solutions lessening the time it takes for a patient to get medical assistance.

User Research

A total of five people was interviewed, with the criteria that all users had to possess experience working with ambulances. All chosen interviewees were intentionally selected with different roles to gain a wider understanding of how different roles within the medical industry interact with ambulances.

To collect more insights from people within the industry an online survey was conducted to reach to a wider audience. Vocalised issues from interviews were used to create the contents of the survey with a total of 47 responses to the survey. The two main issues were resource and time sensitive, previous research on existing technology confirming that these responses were prevalent in daily medical scenarios and not solely limited to extreme cases.

An affinity diagram was created using the information gathered from research, through analysis the data was deciphered into what the core user’s needs were:

  • I want to communicate better with patients.
  • I want to be better equipped.
  • Staff need a better support structure.

Personas

Two personas were created to represent the core users and to confront the issues that were identified with the affinity diagram, creating a better guide to design to their ideal user experience. The storyboard depicts the current experience of one of the personas, illustrating the struggles faced daily.

Conclusion

There is clear potential within the current system for emergency ambulance services to be streamlined. To achieve this further observation is required on communication issues, distribution of equipment/staff as well as a system that can educate its users. Thus, by solving these issues, staff will have more time to care for their patients, giving better aid to both their physical and mental states.

This project was a good challenge, as the first individual research report with a rather large workload. Especially when considering the smaller timeframe given to accomplish this workload, it required greater time managing skills. The research was taken step by step and pre-planned to follow a schedule, changing it into a manageable workflow; thus making it easier to process what had been accomplished and what was still required.
