Evaluation Report

Introduction

The importance of communication between the calling party, paramedics and the dispatchers & non-urgent calls placing strain on the limited resources were the two major themes identified. The solution will address and counter both issues to be effective, with a bigger focus on less urgent medical situations. This creates an environment to allow better allocation of resources for more urgent emergencies to take priority.

Brainstorming

To create these solutions, numerous brainstorming exercises were conducted as a group to compile all major findings and thoughts onto the same page. This assists with identifying any potential design, providing a vast pool of concepts that could potentially be considered or borrow aspects from later in the design process, creating exposure to various possibilities and directions.

Within said session each member chose two ideas that were favoured, including a briefly narrative of its process to the group. Iterative ideation sessions were held to further develop and build upon these potential solutions, contributing additional features onto each design to potentially improve the design; creating potential advantages and limitations. These designs included:

Figure 1.2 - A brainstorm session

  • ambulance drones
  • robotic AI paramedic assistants
  • autonomous decision making AI recognition devices
  • a triaging mobile application
  • a smart ambulance
  • an autonomous equipment allocation system.

The three 'best' ideas were selected after discussion, halving the ideas to:

  • a triaging mobile application
  • a smart ambulance
  • an autonomous equipment allocation system

However the incorporation of the central theme, autonomous technologies, was still minimal for all designs. Through the final brainstorming session, it generated three new ideas. Within those ideas, there was potential to integrate these solution together to create an autonomous system that would benefit both users and emergency service staff.

  • medical chips
  • automatic traffic lights
  • AI recognition street cameras

Sketches & Storyboards

The solution was to create an autonomous ecosystem. To interact with this system, three potential interface ideas were created focusing on the patient's perspective. This was decided as patients are the reason why ambulance services are needed - thus, regular patients and callers are the main users that dictate much of the flow of the system. If less resources were wasted on cases that did not require professional medical attention, the system would ultimately assist emergency service staff.

As the final product aim of this project was to develop an interface, three potential devices said interface would be developed for were chosen:

  • Futuristic street lamps - sensors and cameras to autonomously detect and triage certain injuries in public areas. This solution incorporated features from AI recognition street cameras.
  • A smartwatch application - would use sensors to detect bodily injuries on the wearer. This solution took much influence from the concept of medical chips.
  • Mobile triaging application - would use AI recognition and advanced technology to scan and triage for any injuries or illnesses.

A major flaw in the design of the street lamp was discovered as the main reason behind this idea was to cover emergencies in public areas as a constant presence. However, these devices would not be able to cover private locations due to their nature. There were also ethical issues to consider such as the use of surveillance cameras for triage purposes could potentially breaching one's privacy without consent. Due to these reasons it was abandoned in favour of the other two mediums of technology.

Storyboards provide a visual understanding and explanation of the solution interfaces with user scenarios and respective storyboards drawn up. The flow is easier to interpret in a visual form in comparison to an explanation solely comprised of text.
